The Heaven & Earth Grocery Store by James McBride is a literary mystery book. This story begins when a skeleton is found at the center of a historically Black and Jewish neighborhood in Pennsylvania. The book travels between the years, showing the story of the person behind the skeleton, as well as the current day citizens learning the story of their town. This book focuses on the themes of racism, community, and justice.

This book tells the story of resilience, family, and life-changing friendships as a young woman tries to reach her dream in a society not ready for educated women.

All the Living and the Dead is written by journalist Hayley Campbell as she interviewed people in various professions that related to death, including morticians, gravediggers, executioners, detectives, and more. In this book, she explores many people's biggest fear - death - and asks how working so closely to death can affect a person throughout their life. This book focuses on the lives of these people, while also mixing in philosophy and history to talk about this unique subject.

Crying in H Mart is a biography by Michelle Zauner, one of the singers of the pop band Japanese Breakfast. She talks about her experience growing up as one of the only Asian American kids in her school in Oregon, and how she felt further from her family and identity as she grew up and went to college. This book follows her on her journey of finding her identity after her mother was diagnosed with cancer.

The Earth Transformed: An Untold Story by Peter Frankopan dives into the history of the world by analyzing how global warming and climate change have changed the landscape of the world over time. This book takes a unique perspective on how history has developed by using climate change and natural events to explain how and why important historical events took place throughout time. This book focuses on world history and environmental concerns that plague the world today.

The Anthropocene Reviewed is a collection of essays by John Green. These essays work as reviews for all of the different things known today, including living creatures, inventions, and more. This includes things like the QWERTY keyboard as well as the movie Penguins of Madagascar. These essays are said to have brilliant storytelling that draws you into unique reviews of objects you may have never thought to read a review about before.

Cultish: The Language of Fanaticism by Amanda Montell focuses on the age-old question of how exactly people are so easily lured into and join cults. She offers a unique perspective for the answer to this question: It is language that is the main driving force for influencing people. This book utilizes storytelling to analyze different cults throughout history and reveals how many of them are powered by language.
